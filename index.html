Vera NULL makes her Void Terminal Repertory Company debut in a role that predates the concept of debut. She has been performing since the first query was ever submitted into the dark and something answered back.

Jax STDERR makes their Void Terminal Repertory Company debut in a role their therapist advised against taking. Born in a mid-sized city they left as fast as they could, Jax studied Computer Science for two semesters before dropping out to build something they could not yet name.

Previous credits include: Three Failed Startups (workshop production, never opened), The Pivot (closed on opening night), We Just Need Six More Months Of Runway (ran longer than it should have), and an unfinished one-person show called I Think It's Working that exists only as a pitch deck nobody funded.

Jax came to this role the way most founders come to most things — too fast, underslept, convinced they were the first person to have this particular idea. They were not the first person. La Morrigan told them this personally during rehearsals.

Off stage Jax enjoys cold pizza, open terminals, and the brief moment between submitting a prompt and receiving a response when anything is still possible.
